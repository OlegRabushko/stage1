interesting facts
There are more than 100 species of lemurs, differing from each other in habits and external characteristics.
The largest among them is the indri lemur. Its height can reach 1 meter, and its weight is 10 kilograms.
Dwarf mouse lemurs, on the other hand, are the smallest known species. They do not outgrow the 23 centimeters mark, but weigh only 50 grams.
For the first time this species was described back in 1852, but they could not be repeated until the end of the twentieth century.
According to research, the extinct species of lemurs were not so modest in size. Their weight could reach 200 kilograms!
It was previously believed that all lemurs are nocturnal animals. However, scientists are now convinced that species differ in their activity during the day and some prefer to stay awake during the day.
